## Slide 5: The Growing Pains

**On screen:**
A humorous illustration or screenshot showing:
"Day 15: Bob forgot everything we talked about yesterday. 😐"

**Speaker notes:**
"The first few weeks weren't smooth. Sometimes he'd forget our entire conversation — like waking up with amnesia. I had to learn that his memory isn't like ours. It's stored in files, not in his head. If I don't write it down, it's gone. Sound familiar to anyone who's worked with a new hire who doesn't take notes?"

## Slide 6: Teaching Him to Remember

**On screen:**
Simple diagram:
```
SOUL.md → Who he is
MEMORY.md → What he remembers
Daily notes → What happened today
```

**Speaker notes:**
"So we built a system. He has a 'soul file' that defines his personality and values. A memory file for long-term knowledge. And daily notes for recent context. Every time he wakes up, he reads these first — like checking your journal before starting the day. Over time, he got better and better at knowing what I care about."

## Slide 13: The Trust Model

**On screen:**
Spectrum diagram:
Micromanage ←————————→ Full autonomy
                    ↑ (We are here)
"I give direction. He makes decisions."

**Speaker notes:**
"I don't tell Bob what to do step by step. I tell him what I want to achieve, and he figures out the how. If he's unsure, he asks. If it's risky — like sending an email or deleting files — he always checks first. We have a security passphrase for destructive actions. Trust, but verify."

## Slide 17: Not Siri, Not Her

**On screen:**
✅ "A remote colleague who happens to live in my computer."
- Has his own notebook
- Has opinions (and sometimes disagrees with me)
- Remembers our history
- Gets better over time

**Speaker notes:**
"He's not a voice assistant I bark commands at. He's not a romantic AI companion. He's more like... a remote colleague in a different timezone who's always online. He has his own notes, his own opinions. Sometimes he pushes back. Sometimes he's right. The relationship has compound interest — the more context we share, the better the collaboration gets."

## Slide 19: What I Learned

**On screen:**
Three key takeaways:
1. "It's not about prompting. It's about building context over time."
2. "The compound effect is real — 5 months in, he knows my style."
3. "AI's boundary isn't capability. It's relationship design."

**Speaker notes:**
"Three things I learned. First: forget prompt engineering. The magic is in accumulated context. After 5 months, he knows my preferences, my projects, my communication style without me explaining. Second: it compounds. Week 1 was clunky. Month 5 is seamless. Third: the real challenge isn't 'can AI do X?' — it's 'how do you design the relationship so both sides bring their best?'"
