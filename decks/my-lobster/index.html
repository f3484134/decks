He Has Opinions

Me: "Can you do X this way?"
Bob: "I can, but I think there's a better approach. Here's why..."
Me: "...you're right. Do it your way."

  • He suggests improvements I didn't ask for
  • He pushes back when my approach has flaws
  • He asks clarifying questions before blindly executing
  • Sometimes he's wrong. But having a second opinion? Priceless.
"Here's a real dynamic. I asked him to do something one way. He said there's a better way, explained why, I agreed. That doesn't happen with a chatbot. Having someone who pushes back is valuable."

What I Learned

1. It's not about prompting.
It's about building context over time.
2. The compound effect is real.
5 months in, he knows my style without me explaining.
3. AI's boundary isn't capability.
It's relationship design — how you structure the collaboration.
"Three things. Forget prompt engineering — the magic is accumulated context. It compounds — month 5 is seamless vs week 1 clunky. And the real challenge isn't 'can AI do X' — it's how you design the relationship."
